About SPECPOL

The United Nations General Assembly - Special Political and Decolonization Committee shall be discussing International Territorial disputes with special emphasis on African nations and OPEC nations. The primary purpose of the United Nations is to ensure international cooperation towards establishing global peace and security. A necessary prerequisite for the fulfillment of this very purpose would be equality and harmony among all nations in the world community at large. And for equality to prevail, integrity and sovereignty for all nations must be a reality, not only in theory but in practice. And here at DPSMUN 2016, at the 4th committee of the General Assembly: Special Political and Decolonization Committee, we shall be addressing this very issues that threatens peace for the global community from within itself. To prevent breach of a nation's foremost and basic right. The right to it's own territory. The committee’s dynamics shall also include deliberations upon actions to be undertaken to control local insurgency groups and the various forms of illicit trade which operate with ease in the cross-border domain. As the situation moulds, the AU failing, the world not paying attention, civil wars in several nations, and masses dying, this committee shall be bestowed upon for the task of rehabilitating and re-empowering Africa and the OPEC nations.

Delegates, I would be expecting all of you to be very well researched.

We, at the SPECPOL, will be giving you hard situations to tackle and the way you manage it with the elements of diplomacy, subtlety and quickwit would be the basis of us marking you.

Along with research, what makes a great MUNner different from the others, is the art of convincing. Lobbying will be marked very highly and you can expect the Executive Board's attention towards you at all times of committee.

Coming to one of the most important parts of committee, paperwork will be a major part in the three days. All your lobbying and diplomacy shall narrow down to the final day of paperwork where you get to decide if committee passes or not.

The reason we chose such an agenda is because we feel our circuit hasn't paid much attention to something as important as Territorial Sovereignity in the recent times. With the rise of terrorism and insurgency, the rights of many countries across borders are compromised. As countries resurface from amidst the shackles of civil wars and crises, we decided to talk discuss about one of the most unstable continents at the moment- Africa. Everybody should be thorough with their foreign policy and know what's happening in which country.

At the end of the day what makes one committee better than the others is the quality of debate and rational thinking the delegates possess.

Thank you.

See you all in July.

 

Regards,

Chairperson: Param Vora
